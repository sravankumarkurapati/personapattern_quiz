The Friendship Test: Different Advice Styles

🎓 College Major Crisis

You're feeling overwhelmed about choosing a college major. Three friends give you completely different advice approaches:

📊
Friend A: "The Analyst"
Data-driven, research-focused approach
"Look, you need to research job market trends, salary projections, and industry growth rates. Make a spreadsheet comparing career paths."
💕
Friend B: "The Supporter"
Emotional, experience-based approach
"Dude, I totally get that stress! I changed my major three times. What subjects actually make you excited to learn?"
🌟
Friend C: "The Mystic"
Intuitive, spiritual approach
"Have you considered what the universe is trying to tell you? Maybe meditate on what your soul really wants to do."

Which friend's approach would work best for someone who makes decisions based on facts and research?

Customer Service Persona Styles

📦 Missing Birthday Gift Crisis

Your package was supposed to arrive yesterday for your mom's birthday, but it's lost somewhere. You call customer service and get one of these responses:

🏢
Response A: "The Professional"
Efficient, process-focused
"I apologize for the inconvenience. Let me review your tracking data and escalate this to our logistics team immediately."
💝
Response B: "The Personal Touch"
Empathetic, relationship-focused
"Oh no! Missing your mom's birthday gift - that's so stressful! I'm a mom too, so I totally get it. Let me personally make sure we fix this today."

Both solve your problem equally well. What's the main difference between these responses?
